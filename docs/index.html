How to Read the Dashboard

MERLx IRIS monitors disorder events and hate speech across East Africa. The dashboard has two modes — switch between them using the buttons at the top.

The Disorder Events Wheel

Each dot on the wheel is a disorder event — a disinformation campaign, propaganda operation, hate speech incident, or other narrative that creates societal disruption.

How to read the dots

Bright with glowConfirmed — verified disorder event
Medium opacityPotential — likely but not yet confirmed
FaintContext — background reporting, not necessarily disorder

Threat rings (distance from centre)

The wheel has three concentric rings. An event's position tells you how severe the threat is:

Inner ringP1 CRITICAL — confirmed, high-weight narrative family, multiple escalation signals (high spread, coordinated campaign, VE-linked). Requires immediate attention.
Middle ringP2 HIGH — confirmed with serious narrative family, or coordinated campaign. Should be monitored closely.
Outer ringP3 MODERATE — potential events, context reports, or lower-severity confirmed events.

Wheel segments

The wheel is divided into narrative families — high-level categories like "Ethnic Incitement", "Delegitimization", or "Foreign Influence". Each segment groups events that share the same type of disorder narrative.

Dot size

Larger dots indicate higher spread (how widely the event has been reproduced across platforms) and more observations (how many times the pipeline has detected it).

Connecting lines

Click or hover on a dot to see lines connecting related events. Events are linked automatically based on shared narratives, actors, country, and timing. Unrelated dots dim so you can focus on the cluster.

Dot colours

TealSomalia
EmberSouth Sudan
PurpleKenya
SandRegional / Cross-border

Event types

Events are classified by information type: Disinformation (deliberately false), Misinformation (unintentionally false), Propaganda (coordinated opinion-shaping), Hate Speech (targeted group hostility), Incitement (calls to violence), or Context (background reporting).

The Hate Speech Posts Wheel

Each dot is a single social media post or comment classified by our ML model. This mode shows what types of hate speech are being used online — it's a monitoring feed, not event tracking.

Wheel axes

Posts are arranged by HS subtype — the specific form of hate speech detected:

  • Ethnic Targeting, Clan Targeting, Anti-Foreign
  • Political Incitement, Religious Incitement
  • Dehumanisation, Gendered Violence, General Abuse

Dot colour

RedHate — classified as hate speech
OrangeAbusive — hostile but not hate speech
GreyNormal / Questionable

Distance from centre

Posts closer to the centre have higher toxicity scores — they are more severe. Posts near the edge are lower severity.

How posts are classified

Each post goes through: (1) keyword sweep on social media, (2) ML classification by the EA-HS BERT model, (3) LLM quality check that assigns the subtype, generates an explanation, and flags false positives.

Filters

Use the sidebar on the left to filter by country, event type, information type, or narrative family. Click a filter item to toggle it. Active items have a count showing how many events match.

Timeline

Drag the timeline scrubber at the bottom to filter events by date range. Use the playback controls to watch how the landscape evolved week by week.

Detail panel

Click any dot to see the full detail panel on the right — summary, actors, platforms, linked events, sources, and (for HS posts) the original text with an explanation of why it was classified as hate speech.

Submit

Use the + Submit Post button to manually add a disorder event or hate speech post you've found. It will be processed on the next pipeline run.
